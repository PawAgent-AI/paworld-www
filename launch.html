Launch announcement · April 2026

Paworld is live. One Man Band. 🐾

A pet-AI app, shipped solo — conducted by a team of six silicon engineers.

Download on the App Store ▶ GET IT ON Google Play

Paworld actually shipped internally back in March. I call it my "third project". In early March I open-sourced PawAgent, a pet-focused AI agent. About two weeks in it became obvious that very few people are going to self-host an agent — so I decided to wrap it into an app and get it into real hands.

One person vs. a team that raised ten million dollars

Paworld's natural comparison is Traini, a Silicon Valley pet-AI company that has raised roughly $10M. Since 2022 they've been building vertical models for pet emotion and behavior recognition. Eventually they realized small vertical models get absorbed — or overtaken — by general-purpose foundation models, and they pivoted to a smart-collar hardware play. In a sense, a forced move.

Paworld goes the opposite direction. As an AI agent, it leans all the way into frontier foundation models: recognition quality rides the curve of the underlying model. Today it runs on Gemini (best multimodal at the moment); tomorrow it can swap to whatever is strongest. Don't reinvent the wheel — stand on giants' shoulders.

And all of this was built by one person. Me.

One person, six silicon engineers

From first idea to launch, I did not hand-write a single line of code.

In January I realized AI coding ability had made a step change. In February I started sketching. By March there was a prototype, and in April it launched. I assembled what I think of as an AI engineering team — six silicon engineers, each with a role: A — Product & GTM, B — Agent, C — Backend, D — Mobile, E — Web, F — Admin.

I'd talk product strategy and requirements with A. A would break down tasks for the others. The six of them would then execute in parallel, full throttle, while I QA'd a new build every ten minutes. Absurd iteration speed.

This is what "One Man Band" looks like in 2026: one human conducting an AI orchestra, and a complete product comes out the other end.

Software is about to bloom

The significance of this goes well beyond Paworld itself. The leap in AI coding means a huge class of ideas — ones that previously "weren't worth the engineering cost" — can now be tried for almost nothing. Software, and mobile apps especially, are about to have a Cambrian moment. User needs will be met more thoroughly than ever, and software is becoming a truly competitive market.

My own cadence already backs this up. January and February: projects one and two (in golf). March: project three, Paworld. April: I'm building project four (in automotive, shipping by end of month). May: project five begins.

One project a month. One Man Band.
